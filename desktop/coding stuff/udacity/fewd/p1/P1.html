FrogLand

Washington State and the Pacific Northwest in general is home to many different species of frogs. In the Cascade Mountains and to the west, it is very wet, lush and green, providing many habitats for frogs. Places focused around water and vegetation. East of the Cascades there is more a more arid and dry environment, but provides for different types of and habitats for frogs.

Red-Legged Frog

This is a red-legged frog and is a frog local to the Pacific Northwest. It inhabits cool ponds, lakes, and slow streams.

Pacific Treefrog

This is a Pacific Treefrog. They can come in many colors from grey to light blue to all shades of green and brown. Lives on the ground despite the name.

Great Basin Spadefoot Toad

This is a Great Basin Spadefoot Toad. They are normally a darker color like gray or brown, and tend to be more round. They live east of the Cascade Mountains in Washington.

Cascades Frog

This is a Cascades Frog True to its name, it inhabits the Cascade Mountains in Washington. It looks very similar, but not to be mistaken for the Northern Leopard Frog which lives east of the Cascades.
